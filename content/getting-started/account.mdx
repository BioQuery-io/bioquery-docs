# Creating an Account

BioQuery offers free accounts with limited queries, and expanded access for researchers.

## Why Create an Account?

- **Save Query History** - Access your past queries from any device
- **Share Results** - Generate permalinks to share with collaborators
- **Higher Limits** - Free accounts get 2 queries/day (vs. example-only for anonymous users)

## Sign Up Options

### Google Sign-In (Recommended)

1. Click **Sign In** or **Get Started**
2. Select **Continue with Google**
3. Authorize BioQuery to access your basic profile info
4. You're in!

### Email Sign-Up

1. Click **Sign In** or **Get Started**
2. Select **Sign up with Email**
3. Enter your email address
4. Check your inbox for a magic link
5. Click the link to complete registration

## Managing Your Account

### View Account Settings

1. Click your profile icon (top right)
2. Select **Account Settings**

From here you can manage your profile, research interests, and preferences.

## Your Profile

Set up your research profile to help BioQuery personalize your experience.

### Basic Information

- **Name** - Your display name
- **Institution** - Your university, hospital, or company
- **Role** - Your position (e.g., Postdoc, PI, Industry Researcher)
- **Bio** - Brief description of your research (optional)

### Research Interests

Tell BioQuery what you study:

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| Primary Cancers | Cancer types you focus on (e.g., KIRP, LUAD) |
| Genes of Interest | Genes you frequently study |
| Research Areas | Broader topics (e.g., immunotherapy, biomarkers) |

<Callout type="info">
  Research interests help personalize suggestions in Explorations and filter the Studies feed.
</Callout>

## Query Preferences

Customize how BioQuery responds to your queries:

### Default Baseline

When comparing cancer types, what should be the default comparison?

- **Ask** - Always ask me (default)
- **Pan-Cancer** - Compare across all cancer types
- **Normal Tissue** - Compare to GTEx normal tissue

### Statistical Detail Level

How much statistical detail do you want?

| Level | Shows |
|-------|-------|
| Minimal | p-value, direction only |
| Standard | p-value, effect size, sample counts |
| Comprehensive | Full stats, confidence intervals, power analysis |

### Explanation Style

How should results be explained?

- **Clinical** - Focus on biomarker/therapeutic implications
- **Balanced** - Mix of clinical and technical (default)
- **Technical** - Detailed methodology and statistics

## Onboarding

New users see an onboarding wizard to quickly set up their profile. You can:

- Complete all steps for a personalized experience
- Skip and return later from Account Settings
- Update preferences anytime

## Learned Preferences

BioQuery learns from your usage:

- **Top Genes** - Genes you query most often
- **Top Cancers** - Cancer types you investigate
- **Preferred Analysis Types** - Expression, survival, mutation, etc.

These are displayed (read-only) in your account settings and help improve suggestions.

### Clearing Learned Data

You can clear learned preferences from your account settings. This resets BioQuery's understanding of your research patterns.

## Sign Out

1. Click your profile icon
2. Select **Sign Out**

## Delete Account

To delete your account and all associated data:

1. Go to Account Settings
2. Scroll to **Danger Zone**
3. Click **Delete Account**
4. Confirm deletion

<Callout type="warning">
  Account deletion is permanent. All your query history, projects, explorations, and studies will be deleted.
</Callout>
